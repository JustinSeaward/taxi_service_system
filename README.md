# TAXI_SERVICE

A Python program to track employees, compay expances and revenues.

The context of this problem is that a client has decided to open a small taxi stand 
in their town and asked for your help with the technology. All the brothers
are mechanics so all maintenance will be performed in-house. They have a large garage
between their homes with a small build-out on the side that will be used as an office 
and command center. They have 4 cars that they are using in the business and rent out 
to drivers, while other employees use their own vehicles and pay stand fees.

A text file called Defaults.dat includes some values that will be used by the system.
Values include the Next transaction number (143), the Next driver number (1922), the
Monthly stand fee ($175.00) for drivers with their own car, the Daily rental fee ($60.00)
and the Weekly rental fee ($300.00) for drivers who rent one of the company cars, and
the HST rate (15%).

To keep track of drivers, an Employees table is set up to store driver number, name,
address and phone number, drivers license number and expiry date, insurance policy
company and policy number, a field to represent if they have their own car, and a field
for their balance due – based on stand fees and rentals.

A table was created to record any revenues from the company in a Revenues table. This
table will include a Transaction ID, the date of the transaction, a brief description of the
transaction, the driver number that generated the revenue, the amount of the
transaction, the HST and the Total. Another table was created required to record company 
Expenses for supplies and repairs to the company cars in an Expenses table. This table will 
include an Invoice number, an invoice date, the driver number that generated the expense, 
the item number, description, Cost, the Quantity, and the item total (Cost * Quantity). 
Finally, the Subtotal, the HST and the Total are added.

As drivers rent one of the company cars, we record the information to a Rentals
table. The table includes a rental ID, the driver number, the start date of the rental,
the car number (1, 2, 3, or 4), whether the rental is for a day (from noon to noon the
next day) or a week, the number of days, the rental cost, the HST and the total.

As a driver makes a payment on their balance due, the information is recorded to a Payment
table. This includes a Payment ID, the driver number, the date of the payment, the
amount of the payment, the reason for the payment, and the payment method (Cash,
Debit, or Visa).

You can print each of the following Reports / Receipts that are generated by the system.

  • As a driver rents a company car or makes a payment on their balance, the system
must print a receipt. Make sure it clearly indicates the reason for generating the
receipt.

  • A Profit Listing is a report that is generated based on a Start and End Date –
could be a single day, a month, or the entire year. Includes all revenues and a
total at the end of that part of the listing, then all expenses and a total for that
part of the listing, and finally a calculated Profit (Loss) which is the total revenues
less the total expenses. Included the date range as part of the headings.

  • A Driver Financial Listing can be generated for any driver based on a Start and
End Date. Prompts the user to enter an employee number and includes all entries
from the Revenue table for the driver between the dates specified. Includes
relevant totals at the end of the report.
